[014] <Normal> The manuscript makes three contributions. First, it provides a city-level quantitative assessment of the nonlinear relationship between smart-city construction and carbon emissions, refining the existing smart-city carbon literature that has relied primarily on binary policy evaluations. Second, it identifies an empirically grounded turning point that informs sequencing of smart-city construction across cities with different coordination capacities. Third, it documents associated pathway and moderation mechanisms that connect smart-city construction to industrial upgrading and green technology innovation, offering actionable evidence for urban environmental management.
[015] <Normal> 
[016] <Normal> The empirical design uses an observed panel of 284 Chinese cities from 2006 to 2021, with supplementary instrumental-variable estimation using lagged smart-city construction as an instrument. Robustness checks, regional heterogeneity analysis, and pathway-moderation analyses support the main findings. The study uses publicly available city-level statistical and emissions data and does not involve human participants or animal subjects.
